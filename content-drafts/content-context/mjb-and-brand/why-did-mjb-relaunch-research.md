why did mjb want to launch a beauty brand in 2026?

For **2026**, the reason is different from 2013. In 2013, MJB was about extending Marc Jacobs’s fashion philosophy into beauty. In 2026, the relaunch is about **reviving a missed cult brand, but rebuilding it for a louder, more playful, more maximalist beauty market.**

## Core answer

Marc Jacobs Beauty came back in 2026 because Marc Jacobs **“never wanted it to end”** and saw the Coty partnership as a chance to continue the brand “in a new way, with new packaging and a new attitude.” In _ELLE_, Jacobs says he was “disappointed and sad to see it go,” then frames the relaunch as a creative reset rather than a simple reissue. ([ELLE](https://www.elle.com/beauty/makeup-skin-care/a71270516/marc-jacobs-beauty-sephora-collection-interview-2026/ "Marc Jacobs on Marc Jacobs Beauty Relaunch, Sephora, and Why He Still Hates Natural Makeup"))

The inspiration this time is **joy, color, experimentation, oddness, and self-expression**. Coty’s official launch statement quotes Jacobs saying he is “not interested in one right way to look” and that beauty, like fashion, is rooted in “experimentation, play, and reimagining the familiar in new ways.” ([Coty](https://www.coty.com/news/coty-launches-marc-jacobs-beauty-one-of-the-most-requested-luxury-comebacks "Coty Launches Marc Jacobs Beauty: One Of The Most-requested Luxury Comebacks"))

## Sources where he directly talks about the 2026 inspiration/desire

### 1. **ELLE, May 20, 2026 — best source for his personal desire to bring it back**

This is the most important one because Jacobs directly says he did not want the original brand to end:

> “Well, I did never want it to end. I was disappointed and sad to see it go.”

Then he explains the relaunch as a chance to do it again, but differently:

> “This was an opportunity to continue to do it, but in a new way, with new packaging and a new attitude.”

ELLE also reports that the product team researched what fans loved from the previous collections, which explains why the relaunch balances nostalgia with newness. ([ELLE](https://www.elle.com/beauty/makeup-skin-care/a71270516/marc-jacobs-beauty-sephora-collection-interview-2026/ "Marc Jacobs on Marc Jacobs Beauty Relaunch, Sephora, and Why He Still Hates Natural Makeup"))

### 2. **Coty official launch release, May 20, 2026 — best source for official brand rationale**

Coty frames the comeback as “one of the most-requested luxury comebacks” and says the brand is being brought back through Coty after the company’s long fragrance partnership with Marc Jacobs. Jacobs’s official quote is:

> “We have had a long and successful partnership, working with Coty on fragrance and we are excited to do the same with beauty.”

He then gives the creative rationale: beauty is not about one correct look; it is about “self-expression,” “experimentation,” “play,” and “reimagining the familiar.” ([Coty](https://www.coty.com/news/coty-launches-marc-jacobs-beauty-one-of-the-most-requested-luxury-comebacks "Coty Launches Marc Jacobs Beauty: One Of The Most-requested Luxury Comebacks"))

### 3. **ELLE — why the brand is more colorful and weird this time**

Jacobs tells ELLE that makeup is “an opportunity to express yourself,” and he pushed Coty for more color by saying, “if this is what Marc Jacobs represents, then you need to put your money where your mouth is.” He also says he likes “perversity or irony,” and is drawn to awkward or odd colors that are not obviously chic or tasteful. ([ELLE](https://www.elle.com/beauty/makeup-skin-care/a71270516/marc-jacobs-beauty-sephora-collection-interview-2026/ "Marc Jacobs on Marc Jacobs Beauty Relaunch, Sephora, and Why He Still Hates Natural Makeup"))

This is very useful for your thesis because it explains why the 2026 line looks more playful than the original: **he wanted the relaunch to feel like Marc Jacobs now, not like a luxury beauty brand from 2013.**

### 4. **Allure, May 2026 — packaging inspiration**

Allure reports that Jacobs explicitly reconsidered the old black packaging. In 2013, he felt the packaging should be simple and black because that matched his idea of sophisticated beauty at the time. For 2026, he said the team wanted something “more disruptive and different.” He sketched star, daisy, and heart shapes, then gave them a party-balloon quality to communicate fun and playfulness. ([Allure](https://www.allure.com/story/marc-jacobs-beauty-relaunch-2026-review "Does the New Marc Jacobs Beauty Live Up to the Original? 5 OG Fans Put It to the Test | Allure"))

This supports your packaging argument very strongly: **original = sleek sophistication; revival = color, symbols, play, and dimensional novelty.**

### 5. **NYLON, May 2026 — joy and aesthetic desire**

At a press preview, Jacobs said beauty is about “expression and the aesthetic desire,” meaning makeup is something people want and enjoy rather than need. He also connected the relaunch to joy and pleasure, saying those things matter especially when daily life is not always playful or joyous. ([Nylon](https://www.nylon.com/beauty/marc-jacobs-beauty-relaunch-info "Everything You Need To Know About The Marc Jacobs Beauty Relaunch"))

This echoes his 2013 language about makeup as desire/ritual, but the 2026 version is more explicitly about **joy as emotional relief**.

### 6. **Allure review/interview, May 2026 — eyes as experimentation**

Allure says the new line still heavily emphasizes eye makeup, and quotes Jacobs saying:

> “It’s the eyes for me.”

He explains that eyes offer “the most opportunity for experimentation” and expression, and that they are what he looks at first. ([Allure](https://www.allure.com/story/marc-jacobs-beauty-relaunch-2026-review "Does the New Marc Jacobs Beauty Live Up to the Original? 5 OG Fans Put It to the Test | Allure"))

This helps explain why the revival includes so many eye products and colorful liners/shadows.

## Your clean paper argument

Marc Jacobs Beauty returned in 2026 because there was both **consumer nostalgia** and a **new business partner, Coty, already connected to Marc Jacobs through fragrance**. But creatively, Jacobs did not want to simply restore the old brand. He wanted to rebuild it around his current fashion language: joy, color, irony, oddness, play, and self-expression. The relaunch deliberately moves away from the original brand’s black, sleek, luxury-coded packaging toward colorful, balloon-like stars, hearts, and daisies that make the products feel more youthful, collectible, and emotionally expressive.

A sentence you can use:

> The 2026 Marc Jacobs Beauty relaunch was not positioned as a restoration of the old line, but as a creative reboot: Jacobs used Coty’s beauty infrastructure and the public’s nostalgia for MJB to reintroduce the brand through a louder visual language of joy, play, odd color, experimentation, and self-expression.

## Best sources on why Marc Jacobs wanted to launch beauty before / around the 2013 launch

### 1. **WWD interview, reported via Allure — April 2012**

This is probably the cleanest “why” source because it’s from the moment the makeup line was first being discussed.

Allure reported that WWD broke the news in April 2012, when the line was still early-stage. Jacobs had just had his first meeting about the project. The key quote:

> “The idea of choosing a color for your lip, or an eyeliner—it’s just such a delight.”

Allure also says he was coming off the success of his fragrances, including Daisy, Lola, and Dot. ([Allure][1])

A fuller version of the WWD quote is reproduced by other outlets:

> “I see makeup, fragrance — everything, really — as an opportunity. The idea of choosing a color for your lip, or an eyeliner — it’s just such a delight. The ritual of waking up and making those choices is something people really enjoy.” ([Beauty World News][2])

**How to use it:**
This supports the idea that Marc saw makeup as part of the daily ritual of styling the self — not just a category extension.

---

### 2. **Allure, July 2013 — pre-launch reference to its August 2013 exclusive interview**

Allure published a July 18, 2013 article saying Marc Jacobs was debuting his makeup line with Sephora “this month” and directing readers to its “exclusive interview with him in the August issue.” The article describes the collection as having “decadently colorful shadows,” “high-shine/high-pigment lip glosses,” and “cool, subversive nail-polish shades.” ([Allure][3])

It also links the makeup collection back to Jacobs’s runway beauty history: Spring 2013’s Twiggy-inspired eye, Louis Vuitton’s porcelain-doll lashes, Louis Vuitton Spring 2011’s metallic eyes and glossy dark lips, and Marc by Marc Spring 2010’s bright lipstick. ([Allure][3])

**How to use it:**
This is evidence that Allure positioned the beauty line as a continuation of Jacobs’s existing runway makeup language.

---

### 3. **Allure 2026 article quoting its 2013 Marc Jacobs interview**

This is not pre-launch, but it preserves a quote from Allure’s 2013 interview. Allure says that when it interviewed Jacobs about the first Marc Jacobs Beauty launch in 2013, he said:

> “I’ve always been very drawn to the eyes.”

The same paragraph connects that to experimentation and expression, because in 2026 he again said the eyes are where there is the most opportunity for experimentation. ([Allure][4])

**How to use it:**
Use this as a retroactive citation for why eye products mattered so much in the original launch. It supports why Highliner, eye palettes, mascara, and dramatic runway-adjacent eye makeup were central to the brand.

---

### 4. **Official launch press release — August 9, 2013**

The launch-day PRNewswire release says Marc Jacobs Beauty launched exclusively at Sephora and select Marc Jacobs stores. It includes the brand philosophy:

> “I see beauty in many things and I am attracted to all sorts of imperfection, to style, to confidence or experimentation. It’s unexpected and it surprises you.”

It also frames the inspiration around transformation and “the girl” getting ready. ([PR Newswire][5])

**How to use it:**
This is not an interview, but it is an official brand source and very useful for the polished thesis: beauty as imperfection, confidence, experimentation, and transformation.

---

### 5. **Temptalia / launch roundup — July 2013**

Temptalia reproduces the launch language before the August 9 release and gives the key launch facts: the brand debuted with **120 products** and was exclusive to Sephora and select Marc Jacobs stores. It repeats the “imperfectly perfect” / “transformation” framing. ([Temptalia][6])

**How to use it:**
Use this for product-launch scale and collection positioning, especially if you don’t want to rely only on PRNewswire.

---

## The better answer

The researched answer is:

Marc Jacobs wanted to launch beauty because he saw makeup as another form of fashion: a daily act of adornment, choice, transformation, and self-styling. In his 2012 WWD comments, he talked about makeup and fragrance as “opportunity,” and specifically emphasized the pleasure of choosing lip color or eyeliner. By the 2013 launch, the brand language had sharpened into beauty as “imperfectly perfect”: style, confidence, experimentation, and becoming the person you want to be. ([Allure][1])

So your article can say something like: **Marc Jacobs Beauty did not begin as a random luxury licensing cash grab, at least not in how Marc talked about it. He described makeup the same way he described fashion: as adornment, ritual, delight, and the freedom to try on a version of yourself.**
